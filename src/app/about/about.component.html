About Us

The origins of flight date back to 400 B.C., when the Chinese invented the kite, which was used not only for fun but also to test the weather, and for later development of balloons and gliders. In the 1480s, Leonardo da Vinci was fascinated by the thought of flight and drew pictures and designs of his theories of flight and flying machines. The first recorded flight was in 1783, when two French inventors flew the first hot air balloon, and the Wright brothers successfully flew the first airplane in 1903.
